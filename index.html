Seven metrics. Research-backed. Entirely private.

The question StyleMatch answers: does this submitted piece of writing carry the same stylistic fingerprint as a known sample by the same student? Inspect describes the writing process; StyleMatch compares two pieces of writing.

  • Grammatical Function Words — Burrows' Delta over 30 core function words; among the most stable authorship signals in computational stylistics
  • Discourse Marker Profile — Burrows' Delta over 29 connectives & stance markers; reflects reasoning habits
  • Punctuation Fingerprint — comma, semicolon, dash, colon, !, ? rates per 1,000 characters
  • Avg Sentence Length — characteristic length that persists across writing tasks
  • Sentence Rhythm (SD) — standard deviation of lengths; captures varied vs. uniform rhythm
  • Type-Token Ratio — vocabulary range; interpreted cautiously across different text lengths
  • Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level — reading complexity; stable across tasks for the same writer
  • Pairs naturally with Inspect's Process View report — together they answer how was this written and does it sound like them
  • All computation runs locally — no text ever transmitted
  • Minimum 500 words per sample required

Existence is table stakes. Claim-vs-source is the point.

The question Verify Citations answers: modern misuse rarely invents whole sources — it invents what they said. Real bibliography, invented specifics.

  • Cross-match — bibliography vs. in-text citations, both directions, plus format observations (free, before any tokens are spent)
  • Existence — live web search across journal databases, publisher pages, and Google Books: Located / Not located / Access limited
  • Claim vs. source — quoted passages searched in the reachable text; figures matched exactly; paraphrases compared with both sides quoted
  • Data plausibility — numbers sanity-checked, phrased only as questions
  • Evidence basis stated on every read — full text, abstract, snippet, or description
  • Suggested question per finding for the student conversation
  • Essay text not retained after the report is generated

The essay generates its own defense

The question Oral Defense answers: can the student account for the choices in their own essay — out loud, in their own words, two weeks later? A genuine writer can talk about why they wrote what they wrote.

  • Generate — paste or auto-fill the essay; AI drafts ~10 questions about authorial choices: why this example, why this conclusion, why this structure (1 token)
  • Curate — you are the gatekeeper: keep 4–6, edit any, add your own
  • Send — the student gets a private link; no account, no app (1 token)
  • Defend — each question is hidden until it's read aloud, then 10 seconds to prepare, then recording starts — one take, up to 2 minutes
  • Report — per-question markers (✓ accounted for · ◐ brief · ⚑ couldn't account · — not answered), evidence from the transcript, and suggested follow-ups
  • Transcripts + on-demand audio playback in your dashboard
  • Audio permanently deleted on export (or 30-day backstop); report kept 90 days

An evidence base, not a verdict

Three independent signals. Together, an honest evidence base.

No single tool can determine whether work is authentic. PaperTrail is built around the idea that defensible educator judgment comes from multiple independent sources of evidence — each answering a different question.

📊 Process View FREE

How was this written?

Sessions, paste activity, struggle moments, and writing-process metrics — a printable observational report on how the document was assembled. Free, runs entirely in your browser.

🔍 StyleMatch

Does this sound like them?

Seven stylometric metrics compare the submitted writing to a known sample by the same student. The only PaperTrail tool that compares two pieces of writing against each other.

📑 Source matching External

Did the words come from somewhere?

Tools like Turnitin or your institution's source-matching service answer a question PaperTrail does not — surfacing matches against published sources, prior submissions, and AI-generated text.

A document that is flagged by source-matching, shows stylistic divergence in StyleMatch, AND shows surface editing only in Process View is a different conversation than any of those signals alone. The evidence stack is what makes the conversation defensible.

Verify (AI-assisted close reading), Verify Citations (every source checked against what it actually says), and Oral Defense (the student accounts for their writing, out loud) are further signals available when the situation calls for more — see below.
